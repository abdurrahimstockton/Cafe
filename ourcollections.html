Black Teas

As with all “true teas,” black tea is derived from the Camellia sinensis plant. Leaves of the plant are crushed, curled, rolled, or torn and then left to oxidize before they’re dried and sold. The fact that the leaves are fully oxidized accounts for black tea’s strong, dark flavor profile.


Green Teas

Green tea is prepared from the fresh or withered, lightly heated or steamed leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant. This preparation stops the oxidation process and accounts for green tea’s flavor profile, which is best described as light, fresh, and maybe slightly grassy.


Oolong Teas

Oolong tea is semi-oxidized and is always produced as a whole-leaf tea. The level of oxidation can range widely, and so can the tea’s flavor, color, and aroma. The less oxidized the tea, the lighter it is in color; the more oxidized, the darker the color. It may support heart health.


Pu-erh Teas

Pu-erh tea mostly originates from the Yunnan province of China and may be sold in the form of a brick, cake, or dried leaves. The harvesting process is supposed to bring out the tea’s distinct flavor, which is generally dark, rich, and less astringent than other teas. Pu-erh tea contains antioxidants.
